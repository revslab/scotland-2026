Wait — why are we even discussing this?

We were all set on Islay. Trevor loves Bruichladdich, it's a whisky pilgrimage, done deal. But then I started looking at what we'd be giving up:

🔹 Islay means skipping Edinburgh entirely — not enough days for both

🔹 Islay means an extra flight to coordinate (Loganair, limited schedules)

So before locking it in, I wanted to bring it to the group. The plan is one home base for 4 nights — keep it simple, no hotel-hopping. Here are four options. Each one is a completely different Scotland experience.

☀️ July in Scotland — What to Expect

🌡️ Weather

14–20°C (57–68°F). Scotland's warmest month, but pack layers and a waterproof — rain is always possible. Islay is windier; inland Highlands warmer.

🌅 Daylight

17–18 hours. Sun sets ~10pm and it never gets truly dark. Incredible for exploring. Evenings last forever.

🦟 Midges

Peak midge season, worst in sheltered inland areas (Fort William worst, Islay's coast wind helps). Pack Smidge repellent — the Scottish secret weapon.

👥 Crowds (Jul 5–10 = sweet spot)

Scottish schools break up ~June 30, so local families are out. But English schools don't break until mid-late July — that's when the real UK tourist crush hits. Edinburgh's Fringe isn't until August. Early July is the calmest part of peak season. Still book distillery tours in advance.
