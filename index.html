Wait — why are we even discussing this?

We were all set on Islay. Trevor loves Bruichladdich, it's a whisky pilgrimage, done deal. But then I started looking at what we'd be giving up:

🔹 Islay means skipping Edinburgh entirely — not enough days for both

🔹 Islay means an extra flight to coordinate (Loganair, limited schedules)

🔹 Islay means missing the major beauty of the Highlands — ancient standing stones, dramatic valleys, 5,000-year-old monuments in stunning nature

So before locking it in, I wanted to bring it to the group. The plan is one home base for 4 nights — keep it simple, no hotel-hopping. Here are four options. Each one is a completely different Scotland experience.

Option 2

🌊 Oban

Ancient history + scenery + seafood town

"Seafood capital of Scotland." Charming harbor town on the west coast with the best day-trip radius in the country. Ancient monuments, dramatic valleys, island hopping.

📸 Key Sights
Kilmartin Glen (30 min)
Glencoe Valley (45 min)
Inveraray Castle (45 min)
Highland Coos 🐄
Day Trips (all within 1.5hr)
  • 🪨Kilmartin Glen (30 min) — 350+ ancient monuments, standing stones, 5,000-yr-old carvings
  • ⛰️Glencoe (45 min) — Scotland's most dramatic valley
  • 🏰Inveraray Castle (45 min) — fairytale castle on a loch
  • 🥃Oban Distillery (in town) — one of the oldest in Scotland
  • 🏝️Isle of Kerrera (5 min ferry) — walk to ruined castle, no cars

World-class city + culture + easy day trips

One of Europe's most beautiful cities. Built on volcanoes, packed with history, incredible food scene. The easiest option — no car needed, trains everywhere.

Option 4

⛰️ Fort William

Outdoor adventure + deepest Highland drama

Gateway to the Highlands. Glencoe is 20 minutes away, Ben Nevis towers overhead, and the Harry Potter viaduct is down the road. The most dramatic scenery of any option.

📸 Key Sights
Glencoe (20 min)
Glenfinnan Viaduct (30 min)
Clava Cairns (1.5hr)
Fort William High Street
Day Trips (all within 1.5hr)
  • ⛰️Glencoe (20 min) — right on your doorstep, Scotland's most dramatic valley
  • 🚂Glenfinnan Viaduct (30 min) — the Harry Potter bridge, Jacobite steam train
  • ⭕Clava Cairns (1.5hr) — 4,000-year-old standing stones near Inverness

  • 🌊Steall Falls (15 min + walk) — hidden waterfall in a dramatic valley

☀️ July in Scotland — What to Expect

🌡️ Weather

14–20°C (57–68°F). Scotland's warmest month, but pack layers and a waterproof — rain is always possible. Islay is windier; inland Highlands warmer.

🌅 Daylight

17–18 hours. Sun sets ~10pm and it never gets truly dark. Incredible for exploring. Evenings last forever.

🦟 Midges

Peak midge season, worst in sheltered inland areas (Fort William worst, Islay's coast wind helps). Pack Smidge repellent — the Scottish secret weapon.

👥 Crowds

Peak season. Edinburgh busiest. A82/Glencoe gets traffic. Islay stays quiet. Oban and Kilmartin are middle ground. Book distillery tours in advance.
